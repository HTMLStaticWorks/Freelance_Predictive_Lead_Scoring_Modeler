Why Your Lead Scoring Model is Probably Overfit (And How to Fix It)

Marcus Thornfield
Lead Scoring Consultant

In the world of B2B lead scoring, data leakage is the silent killer of model accuracy. You build a model that shows 99% accuracy on your training data, but once you deploy it to your CRM, the results are mediocre at best.

The Problem with Future Leakage

One of the most common mistakes I see in sales ops data science is including features that only exist *after* a lead has converted. For example, 'Sales Call Duration' might be a great predictor of conversion, but you don't have that data when the lead first enters the system.

# Identifying leaking features
leak_signals = [
    'opportunity_id', 
    'demo_attended_date', 
    'rep_notes_count'
]

# Drop them before training
df_clean = df.drop(columns=leak_signals)
